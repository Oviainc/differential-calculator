Interest Differential Calculator

This is the total amount of your loan or mortgage. For example, if you have a mortgage of $200,000, enter 200000.
Enter the interest rate for the more expensive loan option. You can find this rate on your loan agreement or by checking with your lender. For example, if this rate is 6%, enter 6.
Enter the interest rate for the cheaper loan option. Again, check your loan documents or ask your lender. If this rate is 4%, enter 4.
Enter how many months you want to calculate the interest differential for. For example, if you are comparing over 6 months, enter 6.

Example Calculation

Here’s an example to help you understand how to use this calculator:

If you have:

  • Principal Amount: $200,000
  • Higher Interest Rate: 6%
  • Lower Interest Rate: 4%
  • Number of Months: 6

Your calculation will look like this:

        Interest Differential = Principal × (Higher Rate - Lower Rate) × (Months / 12)
        = $200,000 × ((6%/12) - (4%/12)) × 6
        = $200,000 × (0.005 - 0.00333333) × 6
        = $200,000 × 0.00166667 × 6
        = $2,000

This means you would save $2,000 over 6 months by choosing the lower interest rate.
