Example Calculation

Here’s an example to help you understand how to use this calculator:

If you have:

  • Principal Amount: $200,000
  • Higher Interest Rate: 6%
  • Lower Interest Rate: 4%
  • Number of Months: 6

Your calculation will look like this:

        Interest Differential = Principal × (Higher Rate - Lower Rate) × (Months / 12)
        = $200,000 × ((6%/12) - (4%/12)) × 6
        = $200,000 × (0.005 - 0.00333333) × 6
        = $200,000 × 0.00166667 × 6
        = $2,000

This means you would save $2,000 over 6 months by choosing the lower interest rate.
